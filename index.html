RESOURCES

Magma heats nearby rocks and underground aquifers. Hot water can be released through geysers, hot springs, steam vents, underwater hydrothermal vents, and mud pots. These are all sources of geothermal energy.

More
GEOTHERMAL POWER

Geothermal power is electricity generated from geothermal energy. Geothermal energy is heat from the Earth's core. It comes from the planet's formation and radioactive decay. The heat is stored in rocks and fluids in the Earth's center.

More
GEOTHERMAL ENERGY

Geothermal energy is a renewable energy source because heat is continuously produced inside the earth. People use geothermal heat for bathing, for heating buildings, and for generating electricity.

ENVIRONMENTAL EFFECTS

Geothermal power plants do not burn fuel to generate electricity, but they may release small amounts of sulfur dioxide and carbon dioxide.

TOP GEOTHERMAL POWER PLANTS

PUGA VALLEY POWER PLANT

Puga Valley in Ladakh, India has a geothermal power plant. The valley is located in the Changthang Valley, which is part of the Himalayan geothermal belt.

view

MANIKARAN POWER PLANT

Manikaran, India has an experimental geothermal power plant with a capacity of 5 MW. The plant is located in the Parvati Valley on the river Parvati, in the Kullu District of Himachal Pradesh.

view

TATTAPANI GEOTHERMAL POWER PLANT

The Tattapani geothermal power plant in Chhattisgarh, India is expected to be the country's first geothermal power plant. The plant will use underground hot water springs to generate electricity.

view

JALGAON GEOTHERMAL POWER PLANT

The Maharashtra government has identified Jalgaon district as the country's first geothermal power hub. The state government has identified areas such as Raver, Bhusawal and Savda in the district along the Madhya Pradesh border for the purpose.

View

TAPOVAN GEOTHERMAL POWER PLANT

The Tapovan Vishnugad Hydropower Plant is located on the Dhauliganga River in the Chamoli District of Uttarakhand, India. The 520 MW project is expected to generate approximately 2,558GWh of electricity per year.

View

CHUMATHANG TERMAL POWER PLANT

Chumathang, Jammu and Kashmir has the first geothermal sourced heat pump functional unit in India. T he Puga Chhumathang Geothermal field is useful for binary cycle power generation.
